2nd Chance Blackjack

Every hand deserves another chance!

The rules

Second Chance Blackjack is played very similarly to standard blackjack, with two additional rules for the player:

1) If your hand is busted, you can make a Second Chance Wager.

* The Second Chance Wager is equal to your total wager (including any double).
* You will receive one more card which is subtracted from your total.
* As a Second Chance card, aces count only as one point.
* If you are still over 21, all bets are lost.
* If you win, the main wager pushes and the Second Chance Wager pays 1:1.
* If the player declines to make this wager, their hand busts and loses as normal.

Because of this rule, the player may now strategically hit their stiff hands more often, knowing a bust is not necessarily the end of the hand!


2) Any player total of 21 wins automatically, no matter the dealer hand.

* A player natural always wins, and pays at a 2:1 premium.
* If the player gets a 21 off a Second Chance card, it is resolved as above.

As compensation for the house, the dealer now pushes on all unbusted player hands when drawing a total of 22.

* If the player achieves a total of 21, their win will be paid immediately and will not be pushed by a dealer 22.

Other standard house rules: Dealer hits on soft 17, one card to split aces, any other pair may be split four times, double down on any two cards including after split, surrender is not offered.
